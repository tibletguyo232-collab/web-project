Monthly & Weekly Nutrition Guide

Track your baby's development and nutritional needs throughout your pregnancy journey with our detailed month-by-month and week-by-week guide

Your Pregnancy Progress

Week 6 of 40 - Month 2

Month 1: The Beginning

Weeks 1-4: Conception to Embryonic Development
Week 1: Preparation

Baby Development

Conception occurs. The fertilized egg travels to the uterus for implantation.

Key Nutrients

  • Folic Acid 400-800 mcg
  • Vitamin B6 1.9 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Leafy greens
  • Fortified cereals
  • Lentils

Foods to Limit

  • Raw fish
  • Unpasteurized dairy
  • Excess caffeine

Weekly Tips

Start Prenatals

Begin taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid immediately

Avoid Alcohol

Eliminate alcohol completely from your diet

Hydration

Increase water intake to 8-10 glasses daily

Week 2: Implantation

Baby Development

The fertilized egg implants in the uterine lining. Cell division begins rapidly.

Key Nutrients

  • Folate 400 mcg
  • Vitamin C 85 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Oranges
  • Bell peppers
  • Broccoli

Foods to Limit

  • Raw eggs
  • Soft cheeses
  • Processed meats

Weekly Tips

Balanced Diet

Focus on whole foods, fruits, and vegetables

Cravings

Listen to your body but make healthy choices

Rest

Get 7-9 hours of sleep each night

Week 3: Early Development

Baby Development

The embryo forms three layers that will develop into organs and systems.

Key Nutrients

  • Iron 27 mg
  • Calcium 1000 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Lean red meat
  • Spinach
  • Dairy products

Foods to Limit

  • Swordfish
  • Shark
  • King mackerel

Weekly Tips

Fiber Intake

Add fiber to prevent constipation

Light Exercise

Continue with gentle exercise routines

First Appointment

Schedule your first prenatal visit

Week 4: Confirmation

Baby Development

The neural tube begins forming, which will become the brain and spinal cord.

Key Nutrients

  • Folic Acid 600 mcg
  • Vitamin B12 2.6 mcg

Recommended Foods

  • Eggs
  • Fortified plant milk
  • Nutritional yeast

Foods to Limit

  • High-mercury fish
  • Undercooked meat
  • Unwashed produce

Weekly Tips

Morning Sickness

Eat small, frequent meals to manage nausea

Medications

Review all medications with your doctor

Stress Management

Practice relaxation techniques daily

Month 2: Heartbeat Begins

Weeks 5-8: Major Organs Start Developing
Week 5: Neural Development

Baby Development

The baby's neural tube closes, and the heart begins to beat and pump blood.

Key Nutrients

  • Folic Acid 600 mcg
  • Vitamin B6 1.9 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Fortified cereals
  • Legumes
  • Avocado

Foods to Limit

  • Raw sprouts
  • Deli meats
  • Unpasteurized juice

Weekly Tips

Continue Prenatals

Keep taking prenatal vitamins daily

Small Meals

Eat 5-6 small meals to combat nausea

Listen to Body

Rest when you feel tired

Week 6: Facial Features

Baby Development

The baby's facial features begin to form, and limb buds appear.

Key Nutrients

  • Zinc 11 mg
  • Vitamin D 600 IU

Recommended Foods

  • Nuts and seeds
  • Fortified milk
  • Egg yolks

Foods to Limit

  • Raw shellfish
  • Excess sugar
  • Artificial sweeteners

Weekly Tips

Ginger for Nausea

Try ginger tea or candies for morning sickness

Weight Gain

Expect minimal weight gain this early

Dental Care

Schedule a dental checkup

Week 7: Brain Growth

Baby Development

The baby's brain is growing rapidly, and essential organs continue to develop.

Key Nutrients

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids 200-300 mg DHA
  • Choline 450 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Salmon
  • Walnuts
  • Eggs

Foods to Limit

  • High-sodium foods
  • Fried foods
  • Excess processed foods

Weekly Tips

Safe Fish

Choose low-mercury fish like salmon and cod

Rest

Don't fight fatigue - rest when needed

Hygiene

Wash hands frequently to avoid infections

Week 8: Movement Begins

Baby Development

The baby begins to make spontaneous movements, though you can't feel them yet.

Key Nutrients

  • Protein 75-100 g
  • Vitamin E 15 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Lean poultry
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Leafy greens

Foods to Limit

  • Excess vitamin A
  • Herbal supplements
  • Unregulated health foods

Weekly Tips

Food Safety

Ensure all meats are thoroughly cooked

Vegetables

Wash all fruits and vegetables thoroughly

Prenatal Visit

Attend your first prenatal appointment

Weeks 9-12: Fingers, Toes, and Facial Features Develop

Week 15: Sensory Development

The baby's taste buds are forming, and it can sense light through closed eyelids. The legs are growing longer than the arms.

  • Vitamin A 770 mcg

Recommended Foods

  • Sweet potatoes

Foods to Limit

Week 16: Growth Spurt

Baby Development

The baby is going through a growth spurt. The head is more erect, and the eyes and ears are close to their final positions.

Key Nutrients

  • Iron 27 mg
  • Vitamin C 85 mg

Recommended Foods

  • Lean red meat
  • Citrus fruits
  • Bell peppers

Foods to Limit

  • Tea and coffee with meals (hinders iron absorption)
  • Processed foods with additives
  • High-sugar beverages

Weekly Tips

Anatomy Scan

You may have an ultrasound to check baby's development around this time

Fiber Intake

Increase fiber to prevent constipation common in pregnancy

Skin Changes

Moisturize your growing belly to help with stretching skin
